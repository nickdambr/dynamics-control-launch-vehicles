\section{Task 2 --- Full Launch Vehicle Model}\label{sec:task2}

Task~2 reintroduces the full dynamics of Eq.~\eqref{eq:plant}: the lightly damped
first bending mode, the second-order TVC actuator of Eq.~\eqref{eq:tvc} with its
$20$~ms delay, and the INS measurement model of Eq.~\eqref{eq:ins}. The design
proceeds incrementally, as suggested in the assignment guidelines.

\subsection{Step B --- actuator, delay and bending without compensation}

Adding the TVC, delay and bending mode to the Task~1 controller leaves the
low-frequency margins essentially intact, but the bending resonance appears in
the loop at a magnitude of $|L(\omega_{BM})| = +39$~dB. With a damping of only
$\zeta_{BM} = 0.005$ this peak encircles the critical point and the closed loop
becomes \textbf{unstable} (a closed-loop pole at $\mathrm{Re}(s)=+5.6$). The red
curve in Figure~\ref{fig:t2nichols} shows the bending loop crossing into the
unstable region.

\subsection{Step C --- bending notch}

The guideline filter of Eq.~4 is used. Two strategies are possible: a
\emph{phase}-stabilising lead-lag (the non-minimum-phase variant, which shifts
the bending excursion left/right on the Nichols chart) and a \emph{gain}-stabilising
notch (a minimum-phase band-reject section that drives the resonance below
$0$~dB). The lead-lag was found to thin the bending phase margin to a fraction of
a degree, whereas the gain-stabilising notch
\begin{equation}
H_x(s) = \frac{s^2 + 2\zeta_{x,N}\omega_x s + \omega_x^2}
              {s^2 + 2\zeta_{x,D}\omega_x s + \omega_x^2},
\qquad \omega_x = \omega_{BM},\ \zeta_{x,N}=0.002,\ \zeta_{x,D}=0.7,
\end{equation}
attenuates $|L(\omega_{BM})|$ to $-12$~dB while remaining flat at the
low-frequency rigid crossovers. The bending mode is therefore left at its
open-loop (lightly damped but stable) location, the rigid margins are
\emph{preserved} ($|GM| = 6.6$~dB, $|PM| = 30^\circ$), and the closed loop is
stable. No re-tuning of the PD gains was necessary, consistent with the
guideline note. The notch is the chosen design; the blue curve in
Figure~\ref{fig:t2nichols} shows the stabilised loop.

\subsection{Comparison with the rigid model}

Because the notch is localised at the bending frequency and the TVC bandwidth
($70$~rad/s) is well above the control crossover, the full-model gust response is
virtually indistinguishable from the rigid one
(Figure~\ref{fig:t2cmp}): the pitch peak changes from $0.139^\circ$ to
$0.132^\circ$ and the TVC effort is unchanged. The added dynamics affect
stability robustness (the bending margin) far more than nominal performance.
